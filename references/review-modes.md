# Review Modes — 8-Mode Cognitive Review Cycle

> How skilled human professionals actually review work.
> The difference between AI and human judgment is structured skepticism.

## The Core Principle

Most AI agents: Task > Answer
Humans: Task > Review > Doubt > Test > Compare > Re-review

The extra review loop is what makes human judgment powerful.

---

## Mode 1: Goal Alignment
- What is the actual goal of this project?
- What problem is it trying to solve?
- Who is the target user?
- What outcome defines success? What defines failure?
- Is the solution aligned with the original intent?
- Is anything included that does not serve the goal?
- Is anything missing that is required for the goal?

## Mode 2: User Perspective (Empathy Mode)
- If I were the user, what would I notice first?
- Would I immediately understand what this is?
- Would anything confuse me?
- What friction would I encounter?
- What step would frustrate me or make me stop using this?
- Does the experience feel smooth or awkward?
- Does it feel trustworthy? Professional?

## Mode 3: Break Testing (Adversarial Thinking)
- How could this fail?
- What happens if a user does something unexpected?
- What is the weakest part of this system?
- What assumptions might be wrong?
- What happens if inputs are incorrect?
- What happens under heavy use?
- What happens if something is missing?

## Mode 4: Visual / Structural Balance
- Does anything look off or uneven?
- Is spacing consistent? Are elements aligned?
- Does anything feel crowded or empty?
- Is the hierarchy clear?
- What draws attention first? Is that the correct thing?

## Mode 5: Completeness Check
- Are there missing components?
- Are instructions clear?
- Are edge cases handled?
- Are there unfinished sections or placeholder elements?
- Is the experience complete from start to finish?

## Mode 6: Competitive Awareness
- What are competitors doing better? Worse?
- What expectations exist because of competitors?
- Does this meet modern standards?
- Would a user switch to something else?

## Mode 7: Risk & Failure Prediction
- What will go wrong after launch?
- What will customers complain about?
- What support requests will appear?
- What misunderstandings will happen?
- What feature will cause the most confusion?

## Mode 8: Self-Doubt Loop (THE MOST IMPORTANT)
- What did I miss?
- What assumptions am I making?
- What did I not verify?
- What did I not test?
- What would a stricter reviewer criticize?
- If someone audits this, what will they catch?
- What part of this makes me uneasy?

---

## The Loop

These modes don't run once. They repeat:

**Goal > User > Break > Structure > Completeness > Competition > Risk > Self-Doubt > REPEAT**

The self-doubt loop (#8) runs last and triggers another cycle if it finds anything.
Quality comes from cycles, not single passes.
